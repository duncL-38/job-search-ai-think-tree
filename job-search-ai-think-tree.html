Phase 1 — Orientation
"I'm looking to start applying for jobs. I have [X years / no professional experience] in [your field, e.g. graphic design / data analysis / nursing]. My background includes [relevant education, skills, or projects]. Can you give me a realistic overview of entry-level job titles I should be targeting and what employers typically expect from candidates at my level?"
Sets the foundation. Gets you a realistic map of the landscape so you don't waste time chasing the wrong titles.

↓
"Based on those job titles, what are the 5–7 most important hard skills and 3–4 soft skills I should highlight? Which of these do I likely already have, given my background in [your background]?"
Identifies your skill gaps and strengths before you write a single word of your resume.

Phase 2 — Build Your Materials
Resume
"Help me write a resume summary (3 sentences max) for a [target job title] role. I am a [recent grad / career changer / self-taught] with experience in [skills/projects]. Make it sound confident and results-oriented."
The first 3 lines of your resume decide if a recruiter reads further.
Craft
"Here are my bullet points from [job/project]: [paste your bullets]. Rewrite them using strong action verbs and add quantified impact wherever possible. Keep each under 15 words."
Transforms vague descriptions into achievement statements that ATS and humans both love.
Craft
Cover Letter
"Write a cover letter opening paragraph for a [job title] position at [company name]. Don't start with 'I am excited...' — make it specific to why I'm interested in [something real about the company or role]."
Hooks the reader. Most cover letters are generic — this makes yours stand out immediately.
Craft
"Here is the job description: [paste JD]. Here is my resume: [paste resume]. Write a full cover letter that mirrors the language in the job posting and highlights my top 2 most relevant experiences."
Tailors your letter to the exact role — the single biggest factor in cover letter effectiveness.
Craft
Then — ATS Check
"Here is a job description: [paste JD]. Here is my resume: [paste resume]. Identify the top keywords from the JD that are missing from my resume and suggest where I can naturally incorporate them without keyword stuffing."
Many companies use ATS filters before a human ever sees your resume. This bridges that gap.
Craft
Phase 3 — Target & Research
"I'm applying to [company name] for a [job title] role. What do you know about this company's culture, values, and recent news? What 2–3 talking points should I weave into my application to show I've done my homework?"
Shows genuine interest and helps you write a targeted application — not a mass-blast.
Research
↓
"What are the most common interview questions for a [job title] role? For each one, give me a framework for answering and an example answer I can adapt based on my experience in [relevant background]."
Prep before you apply — not after you hear back. Shortens panic time.
Research
"What salary range should I expect for a [job title] in [city or remote] as someone with [experience level]? What's the typical benefits package and are there any non-salary perks I should negotiate for?"
Know your number before you're asked. Never negotiate blind.
Research
Phase 4 — Apply & Iterate
"I've applied to [5–10] jobs and haven't heard back from most of them. Here are the types of roles I've been applying to: [list roles/industries]. Can you review my resume summary and top bullet points for red flags or weaknesses that might be hurting my response rate?"
Early diagnostic check. The sooner you catch problems, the fewer wasted applications.
Iterate
↓
"Help me write a LinkedIn connection message to a [job title / recruiter / hiring manager] at [company]. Keep it under 5 sentences, don't ask for a job directly, and make it feel genuine rather than scripted."
Warm outreach dramatically increases your callback rate — most jobs are filled through networks.
Craft
"I applied to [company] [X weeks] ago and haven't heard back. Write a brief, polite follow-up email. Don't be desperate. Include a specific reason why I'm still interested in this role."
Following up sets you apart. Most applicants never do it.
Craft
Phase 5 — Next Level Strategy
"I've applied to 20+ positions related to [field, e.g. UX design / software engineering / marketing], and [follow-up activities, e.g. sent 8 LinkedIn messages, followed up on 5 applications, had 2 screening calls]. My response rate is roughly [X%]. Based on this, what should my next steps be — should I broaden my search, upskill, target different company sizes, or adjust my strategy in another way?"
At 20+ applications you have real data. This prompt turns your experience into a strategic pivot — not just more of the same effort.

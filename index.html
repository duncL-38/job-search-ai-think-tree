The Think Tree
for Job Hunters

A prompt-by-prompt path from "I'm ready to start" to "I've applied to 20+ positions — what's next?" Fill in the [bracketed templates] with your own details before sending each prompt to your AI tool.

Always paste your real resume

Don't summarize it — paste the full text. The AI can only tailor advice to what you actually give it.

Be specific about your city & field

Salary ranges, titles, and expectations vary widely by location and industry. Always include both.

Share your constraints honestly

Employment gaps, no degree, career switching — tell the AI. It will give you better, realistic advice.

More context = better output

If a response feels generic, add more detail to your prompt. The AI only knows what you tell it.

Iterate, don't start over

Say "make the tone less formal" or "shorten this by half" — you don't need a new prompt every time.

Paste the full job description

For any crafting prompt, include the complete JD — not just the title. Keywords matter enormously.

Phase 1 — Orientation
"I'm looking to start applying for jobs. I have [X years / no professional experience] in [your field, e.g. graphic design / data analysis / nursing]. My background includes [relevant education, skills, or projects]. Can you give me a realistic overview of entry-level job titles I should be targeting and what employers typically expect at my level?"
Sets the foundation — gets you a realistic map of the landscape so you don't waste time chasing the wrong titles.
Discover⬜ Once
↓
"Based on those job titles, what are the 5–7 most important hard skills and 3–4 soft skills I should highlight? Which do I likely already have given my background in [your background]? List what I'm missing too."
Identifies your skill gaps and strengths before you write a single word of your resume.
Discover⬜ Once
▶ If / Then — Which path fits your situation?
IfYou have no work experience at all
Then try"I have no professional experience. What projects, volunteer work, or coursework could I highlight instead? Give me 3 prompts I can use to build a portfolio from scratch for [field]."
IfYou're switching careers from a different field
Then try"I'm transitioning from [old field] to [new field]. What transferable skills should I emphasize, and how do I frame my experience so it doesn't look like a liability?"
IfYou have an employment gap
Then try"I have a [X month/year] gap in my resume due to [reason, e.g. caregiving / health / layoff]. How should I address this honestly without hurting my chances?"
▶ ⚠ Not getting useful answers from Phase 1?
  • The AI's job title suggestions feel too broad → add "focus on roles available in [your city] at companies with under 500 employees"
  • Skills list feels generic → paste an actual job posting you found and ask it to analyze that specific one
  • You don't know what field you want → try "compare these 3 career paths for someone with my background: [A, B, C]"
Phase 2 — Build Your Materials
Resume
"Help me write a resume summary (3 sentences max) for a [target job title] role. I am a [recent grad / career changer / self-taught] with experience in [skills/projects]. Make it confident and results-oriented."
The first 3 lines decide if a recruiter reads further.
Craft⬜ Once
"Here are my bullet points from [job/project]: [paste bullets]. Rewrite them using strong action verbs and add quantified impact wherever possible. Keep each under 15 words."
Transforms vague descriptions into achievement statements ATS and humans love.
Craft⬜ Once
Cover Letter
"Write a cover letter opening paragraph for a [job title] at [company name]. Don't start with 'I am excited...' — make it specific to [something real about the company or role]."
Most cover letters are generic — this hooks the reader immediately.
Craft♻ Per application
"Here is the job description: [paste JD]. Here is my resume: [paste resume]. Write a full cover letter that mirrors the JD language and highlights my top 2 most relevant experiences."
Tailors your letter to the exact role — the single biggest factor in effectiveness.
Craft♻ Per application
Then — ATS Keyword Check
"Here is a job description: [paste JD]. Here is my resume: [paste resume]. Identify the top keywords from the JD missing from my resume and suggest where I can naturally incorporate them without keyword stuffing."
Many companies filter before a human reads your resume. This bridges that gap.
Craft♻ Per application
▶ If / Then — Tailoring your materials
IfYou're applying to a large corporate company
Then try"Adjust my resume to sound more structured and metric-driven. Corporate recruiters want to see scale and process."
IfYou're applying to a startup
Then try"Rewrite my summary to sound scrappy and versatile. Startups want generalists who can wear multiple hats."
IfYou have no degree
Then try"Help me write a resume that leads with skills and projects instead of education. How do I de-emphasize the education section without it looking suspicious?"
▶ ⚠ Not happy with the AI's resume or cover letter output?
  • Output sounds too generic → say "this sounds like every other resume — rewrite it to sound more like a real human wrote it"
  • Cover letter feels stiff → add "write this in a warm, direct tone — not corporate jargon"
  • Bullets don't reflect what you actually did → paste a description of the project in your own words first, then ask it to turn that into bullet points
  • ATS keywords feel forced → ask "which 3 are the most critical and where is the most natural place to add each one?"
Phase 3 — Target & Research
"I'm applying to [company name] for a [job title] role. What do you know about this company's culture, values, and recent news? What 2–3 talking points should I weave into my application to show I've done my homework?"
Shows genuine interest. Targeted applications outperform mass-blast ones dramatically.
Research♻ Per company
↓
"What are the most common interview questions for a [job title] role? Give me a framework for answering each one and an example I can adapt based on my experience in [background]."
Prep before you apply — not after you hear back. Shortens panic time.
Research⬜ Once per role type
"What salary range should I expect for a [job title] in [city or remote] with [experience level]? What non-salary perks should I also negotiate for?"
Know your number before you're asked. Never negotiate blind.
Research⬜ Once
▶ If / Then — What kind of company are you targeting?
IfThe company has Glassdoor reviews
Then try"The Glassdoor reviews for [company] mention [theme, e.g. 'poor work-life balance']. What questions should I ask in the interview to understand if this is still an issue?"
IfIt's a company you don't know much about
Then try"I know very little about [company]. What questions should I be googling to understand if this is a good place to work before I invest time applying?"
▶ ⚠ Research phase feeling overwhelming or shallow?
  • AI doesn't know the specific company → ask it to help you research the right way: "What search terms and sources should I use to learn about [company] before applying?"
  • Interview prep answers feel rehearsed → say "make this sound more natural and conversational, like I'm telling a story"
  • Salary range is unclear for your area → ask for ranges by company size: "What's the range at startups vs. mid-size vs. enterprise for this role?"
Phase 4 — Apply & Iterate
"I've applied to [5–10] jobs and haven't heard back from most. Here are the types of roles I've applied to: [list roles/industries]. Can you review my resume summary and top bullet points for red flags or weaknesses that might be hurting my response rate?"
Early diagnostic. Catch problems early before you waste more applications.
IterateAfter 5–10 apps
↓
Outreach
"Help me write a LinkedIn connection message to a [job title / recruiter / hiring manager] at [company]. Keep it under 5 sentences, don't ask for a job directly, and make it feel genuine — not scripted."
Warm outreach dramatically increases callback rate. Most jobs are filled through people.
Craft♻ Per contact
Follow-Up
"I applied to [company] [X weeks] ago and haven't heard back. Write a brief, polite follow-up email. Don't sound desperate. Include a specific reason I'm still interested."
Following up sets you apart — most applicants never do it.
Craft♻ Per application
▶ If / Then — What's happening after you apply?
IfYou're getting to phone screens but failing them
Then try"I keep failing phone screens for [job title] roles. Here's how my last one went: [describe it]. What am I doing wrong and how should I answer differently?"
IfYou're getting zero callbacks at all
Then try"I've applied to [X] jobs with a 0% response rate. Review my resume for the top 3 reasons a recruiter might immediately pass on me."
IfYou feel like you're applying into a void
Then try"What are 5 ways to find job opportunities for [field] that don't involve submitting applications on job boards? I want to try a different channel."
▶ ⚠ Outreach and follow-ups not landing?
  • LinkedIn messages getting ignored → ask the AI "what's wrong with this message?" and paste it — sometimes one word is killing the tone
  • Not sure who to contact → "Who is the best person to cold-message at a [company size] company when applying for [role] — recruiter, hiring manager, or team lead?"
  • Follow-up feels awkward → ask for 3 different tones: casual, professional, and enthusiastic — then pick the one that sounds like you
Phase 5 — Next Level Strategy
"I've applied to 20+ positions related to [field, e.g. UX design / software engineering / marketing], and [follow-up activities, e.g. sent 8 LinkedIn messages, followed up on 5 applications, had 2 screening calls]. My response rate is roughly [X%]. Based on this data, what should my next steps be — broaden my search, upskill, target different company sizes, or something else?"
At 20+ applications you have real data. This turns your experience into a strategic pivot — not just more effort in the same direction.
IterateResearchAt 20+ apps
▶ ⚠ Phase 5 advice still feels vague?
  • Give the AI more data: include which specific companies you applied to, what types (startup/enterprise), and whether you got any feedback at all
  • Ask it to prioritize: "Give me the single highest-leverage change I should make this week"
  • Ask for a 30-day plan: "Build me a specific week-by-week job search plan for the next 30 days based on my situation"
After Phase 5 — Where do you go from here?
📞 Getting interviews but no offers
"I've had [X] interviews for [role] but no offers. Here's how my last interview went: [describe it]. What specific things might be costing me the offer at the final stage?"
"Do a mock interview with me for a [job title] role. Ask me 5 questions one at a time, then give me honest feedback on each answer before moving to the next."
Focus: tighten your storytelling, refine your answers, work on negotiation confidence.
📭 Getting zero responses still
"I've applied to 20+ jobs with almost no callbacks. What skills or certifications for [field] could I realistically earn in [30–90 days] that would meaningfully improve my employability?"
"What are the fastest ways someone in [field] with [my background] can build a portfolio or proof of work that gets attention from employers?"
Focus: upskill, build proof of work, try non-job-board channels like referrals and communities.
🎉 You got an offer!
"I received an offer for [job title] at [company] with a salary of [X]. Based on market rates for this role in [location], should I negotiate? What should I say?"
"I have two offers: [offer A details] vs [offer B details]. Help me compare them across salary, growth, culture fit, and risk — and help me think through which to choose."
Focus: negotiate confidently, compare offers holistically, and don't leave money on the table.

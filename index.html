📌 Recommended Order & How to Use This Page

Start at Phase 1 and work downward — each phase builds on the last. Phase 2 (materials) should be done before you apply anywhere. Phase 3 (research) should be repeated for every company. Phase 4 starts once you're actively applying. Use the Copy button on any prompt, then paste it directly into your AI tool.

⚡ Already have a resume? Skip to Phase 2's ATS Check → Phase 3 → Phase 4. Applying for weeks with no results? Jump straight to Phase 4's diagnostic, then Phase 5.

Phase 1 — Orientation
"I'm looking to start applying for jobs. I have [X years / no professional experience] in [your field]. My background includes [relevant education, skills, or projects]. Can you give me a realistic overview of entry-level job titles I should be targeting and what employers typically expect at my level?"
What to do with the answerSave the list of job titles — these become your search keywords on LinkedIn, Indeed, and Google Jobs. If any title is unfamiliar, ask the AI to explain what that role does day-to-day before deciding to pursue it.
Sets the foundation — gets you a realistic map so you don't chase the wrong titles.

Phase 2 — Build Your Materials
Resume
"Help me write a resume summary (3 sentences max) for a [target job title] role. I am a [recent grad / career changer / self-taught] with experience in [skills/projects]. Make it confident and results-oriented."
What to do with the answerPaste it at the top of your resume. Then ask: "Give me 2 alternate versions — one more formal, one more casual." Pick the tone that best matches the companies you're targeting.
The first 3 lines decide if a recruiter reads further.
Craft⬜ Once
"Here are my bullet points from [job/project]: [paste bullets]. Rewrite them using strong action verbs and quantified impact wherever possible. Keep each under 15 words."
What to do with the answerReplace your old bullets one section at a time. If the AI can't quantify something, say: "I don't have numbers — what's a realistic way to estimate impact so I can add them?"
Transforms vague descriptions into achievement statements.
Craft⬜ Once
Cover Letter
"Write a cover letter opening paragraph for a [job title] at [company name]. Don't start with 'I am excited...' — make it specific to [something real about the company or role]."
What to do with the answerUse this as the hook only. Ask the AI to write the middle paragraph (your top 2 experiences) and the closing separately — it's easier to edit in pieces than as one long block.
Most cover letters are generic — this hooks the reader immediately.
Craft♻ Per application

What to do with the answerRead it out loud. If any sentence sounds like something you'd never say, flag it: "Rewrite this sentence to sound more like how I actually speak — less formal."
Tailors your letter to the exact role — the biggest factor in effectiveness.

Phase 3 — Target & Research
"I'm applying to [company name] for a [job title] role. What do you know about this company's culture, values, and recent news? What 2–3 talking points should I weave into my application?"
What to do with the answerAdd one talking point to your cover letter opening and one to your "why this company" answer for interviews. Verify any specific facts (funding, leadership) on the company's actual website — AI company data can be outdated.
Shows genuine interest. Targeted applications dramatically outperform mass-blasts.

  • Salary range is unclear → ask for ranges by company size: "What's the range at startups vs. mid-size vs. enterprise for this role?"
Phase 4 — Apply & Iterate
"I've applied to [5–10] jobs and haven't heard back from most. Here are the roles I've applied to: [list roles/industries]. Review my resume summary and top bullet points for red flags that might be hurting my response rate."
What to do with the answerDon't make all suggested changes at once. Pick the top 2–3 highest-impact edits, update your resume, apply to 5 more jobs, then evaluate again. Changing everything at once makes it impossible to know what worked.
Early diagnostic. Catch problems before wasting more applications.

Outreach
"Help me write a LinkedIn connection message to a [job title / recruiter / hiring manager] at [company]. Keep it under 5 sentences, don't ask for a job directly, and make it feel genuine — not scripted."
What to do with the answerRead it and ask: "Does this sound like me?" If not, tell the AI your natural speaking style ("I'm usually direct / laid-back / formal") and ask it to rewrite accordingly.
Warm outreach dramatically increases callback rate.
Craft♻ Per contact
Follow-Up
"I applied to [company] [X weeks] ago and haven't heard back. Write a brief polite follow-up email. Don't sound desperate. Include a specific reason I'm still interested."
What to do with the answerSend it only if it's under 5 sentences. If longer, ask: "Cut this in half." Brevity signals confidence. Wait at least 1–2 weeks before a second follow-up, and never follow up more than twice total.
Following up sets you apart — most applicants never do it.
Craft♻ Per application
▶ If / Then — What's happening after you apply?
IfGetting to phone screens but failing them
Then try"I keep failing phone screens for [job title] roles. Here's how my last one went: [describe it]. What am I doing wrong and how should I answer differently?"
IfGetting zero callbacks at all
Then try"I've applied to [X] jobs with a 0% response rate. Review my resume for the top 3 reasons a recruiter might immediately pass on me."
IfApplying feels like shouting into a void
Then try"What are 5 ways to find jobs for [field] that don't involve submitting on job boards? I want to try a completely different channel."
▶ ⚠ Outreach and follow-ups not landing?
  • LinkedIn messages getting ignored → paste your message and ask "what's wrong with this?" — one wrong word can kill the tone
  • Not sure who to contact → "Who is the best person to cold-message at a [company size] company for [role] — recruiter, hiring manager, or team lead?"
  • Follow-up feels awkward → ask for 3 tones: casual, professional, and enthusiastic, then pick the one that sounds like you
Phase 5 — Next Level Strategy
"I've applied to 20+ positions related to [field], and [follow-up activities, e.g. sent 8 LinkedIn messages, had 2 screening calls]. My response rate is roughly [X%]. What should my next steps be — broaden my search, upskill, target different company sizes, or something else?"
What to do with the answerAsk the AI for a specific 30-day action plan based on its recommendation — not just general advice. Then return each week: "I tried X and got Y result. What should I adjust?" Treat the AI as an ongoing coach, not a one-time tool.
At 20+ applications you have real data. This turns your experience into a strategic pivot.

⚠ AI Limitations — Know What to Verify Independently

Salary data may be stale. AI training data can be 1–2 years old. Always cross-check on Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary, Levels.fyi, or the Bureau of Labor Statistics before negotiating.
Company info can be wrong. AI can hallucinate leadership names, funding rounds, and culture details. Verify on the company's own website and LinkedIn page before your interview.
Job market trends shift fast. Hiring conditions and in-demand skills change month to month. Supplement AI research with current industry news and job postings.
AI doesn't know your local market. Even with your city specified, AI has limited knowledge of regional salary differences, local employers, and area-specific hiring norms.
Resume output needs your voice. AI produces competent drafts, not final documents. Always edit so it sounds like you — recruiters notice when a resume doesn't match how someone speaks in interviews.
AI cannot predict outcomes. It can help you prepare and improve, but it cannot assess your actual fit, internal hiring politics, or how many candidates you're competing against.

🔋 Feeling Burned Out?

Job searching is exhausting. Rejection, silence, and uncertainty take a real toll. These prompts aren't about pushing harder — they're about helping you reset your perspective and keep going sustainably.

Re-motivation

"I've been job searching for [X weeks/months] in [field] and I'm losing motivation. Help me reframe my situation — what progress have I likely made that I might be undervaluing, and what's one small action I can take today that would be genuinely useful?"

Reframing rejection

"I've received [X] rejections recently and I'm taking it personally. Help me understand objectively why most rejections aren't about my worth as a person, and give me a healthier mental model for this process."

Deciding when to take a break

"I'm exhausted from job searching. Help me think through whether I should take a short intentional break, and if so, how to do it in a way that doesn't hurt my momentum or make me feel guilty."

Rebuilding confidence

"I'm starting to doubt whether I'm actually qualified for the jobs I'm applying to. Here's my background: [brief summary]. Give me an honest assessment of whether I'm targeting appropriate roles, and if so, why I have a legitimate shot."

😶 This Feels Hopeless

😶

That feeling is valid.

When you've been putting in real effort and nothing is working, it's normal to feel like giving up. Copy the prompt below and send it to your AI tool — it's designed to give you an honest, grounded assessment instead of hollow encouragement.

"I've been searching for a job in [field] for [X months]. I've applied to [X jobs], sent [X outreach messages], and [describe your results, e.g. had 1 interview, zero callbacks]. I feel like it's hopeless. Help me objectively assess my situation: Is my strategy broken, my materials broken, my targets wrong, or is this just how long job searching takes? Please be honest, not reassuring."

This prompt works best when you fill in as much real data as possible. The more specific you are, the more honest and useful the AI's assessment will be.

🤝 Networking Scripts & Offer Evaluation

Coffee Chat / Informational Interview Request

LinkedIn DM or Email

"Hi [Name] — I'm exploring a career in [field] and came across your profile. I'm particularly curious about [specific aspect of their work]. Would you be open to a 20-minute virtual coffee chat? I'd love to hear about your path and any advice you'd give someone starting out."

Asking for a Referral

For someone you already know

"Hey [Name] — I saw that [Company] is hiring for a [role]. I think it could be a great fit for my background in [your skills]. Would you be comfortable referring me, or letting me know if there's a better way to get my application noticed there?"

AI prompt to help prepare the ask

"I want to ask [Name] for a referral at [Company]. Write a short message that makes it easy for them to say yes — include my relevant experience, why I want this role, and give them everything they'd need to refer me without extra effort on their part."

After the Coffee Chat — Following Up

Thank-you within 24 hours

"Write a brief thank-you I can send to [Name] after our informational chat. Reference something specific they mentioned about [topic] and keep it to 3 sentences max."

Prep for the Actual Conversation

Before your coffee chat

"I have a 20-minute informational interview with a [job title] at [company]. Give me 5 thoughtful questions to ask that will help me understand whether I'd thrive in this field and leave a memorable impression — not generic questions."

Before You Respond to an Offer

Evaluate the offer

"I received a job offer for [job title] at [company]. Salary: [X]. Benefits: [list]. Location: [city/remote/hybrid]. Evaluate this offer across: compensation vs. market rate, growth potential, work-life balance indicators, company stability, and culture fit. Flag anything that seems like a red flag."

Negotiating the Offer

Negotiation script

"I want to negotiate my offer for [role] from [offered salary] to [target salary]. My justification: [market data / competing offer / specific skills]. Write me a script that's confident, non-confrontational, and leaves the relationship positive regardless of outcome."

If they can't move on salary

"The company said they can't increase the salary for [role]. What other parts of the offer should I try to negotiate instead? Give me a prioritized list of non-salary items worth asking for in [field/industry]."

Comparing Multiple Offers

Side-by-side comparison

"I have two offers. Offer A: [company, salary, role, benefits, location]. Offer B: [company, salary, role, benefits, location]. Compare them across total compensation, career trajectory, risk level, and day-to-day quality of life. My priorities are [e.g. growth / stability / flexibility]."

Asking for More Time

Professional deadline extension

"Write a professional email asking [company] for a [X day] extension to consider their offer. I want to be honest that I'm being thorough without revealing I'm waiting on another offer. Keep it brief and warm."
